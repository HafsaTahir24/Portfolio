Visual Learning

Visual Learning is a learning style in which a person learns through visual aids such as charts, graphs, diagrams, and more. Visual learners rely on seeing and observing in order to understand and learn new information. They may have difficulty with verbal instructions and may benefit from seeing information presented in a visual format. Visual learners often benefit from using color-coded notes, flashcards, and other visual aids to help them remember information. They may also benefit from creating visual representations of information, such as mind maps and diagrams. Visual learners are good at remembering faces and places, and may benefit from using visual cues to help them remember information. Visual learners may also benefit from using visual association techniques to help them remember information.
